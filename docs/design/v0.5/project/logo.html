Directions

Each mark is built on a 24-unit grid with a 2-unit stroke. The recommended direction earns the K-form by drawing it as the proxy itself — many requests funnel into a single bar.

Convergence K 01
The K is the proxy. Vertical bar = data plane. Two diagonals = upstreams funneling in and clients routing out. Accent dot marks the convergence — the only chromatic mark in the system, reads as a status LED.
Aperture 02
Gateway reading: the outer square is the perimeter, the K-shape inside is the path through it. Strong at favicon size; less distinctive than 01.
Refraction 03
Prism splits one beam into many — visually literal for what a reverse proxy does. Beautiful, but reads as graphics software at small sizes. Use as a marketing mark, not the app icon.

Wordmark 04
For docs, CLI banners, the OSS readme. All-lowercase Inter at -0.03em tracking. The terminal-cursor dot is the only chromatic element — same accent dot that lives in the Convergence K.

Construction · primary mark

The Convergence K is drawn on a 96-unit canvas with a 7-unit stroke, 14-unit safe area, and exact 45° diagonals. The convergence node is 8×8 — the only solid mass in the mark.

Why this mark

The K names the product but the geometry tells you what it does. Two paths converge into one bar, the convergence is highlighted by the only colored pixel in the system. That node also doubles as a status indicator at the top of the sidebar — green when the data plane is healthy, amber while reloading config, red on failure.

  • Stroke: 7 units on a 96 grid (~7.3%)
  • Diagonals: exactly 45°, meeting the bar at midpoint (22, 48)
  • Convergence node: 8×8, centered on the meeting point
  • Safe area: 14 units on all sides (14.6% padding)
  • Min legible size: 16 px favicon (tested)
